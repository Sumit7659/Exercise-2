Favourite Sports

  1. Cricket
    1. Virat Kohli
      • Virat Kohli is the only Indian batsman who got 890 rating points in the ICC ODI ranking. Previously Sachin Tendulkar got the best rating of 887 in 1998.
      • Virat Kolhi has the record of having the highest ICC rating points (922) in the ICC Player Rankings for Test Batsmen.
      • Virat Kohli scored six double centuries as a captain in test matches. He surpassed Cricket legend Brian Lara, who had five double centuries as a test captain.
      • Virat Kohli has the most number of 150+ scores as captain in Tests – 9 times.
    2. MS Dhoni
      • First Indian and fifth overall player to hit 200 sixes in ODIs
      • In 2005, Dhoni scored 183* against Sri Lanka which is the highest score by a wicket-keeper.
      • He holds the current record of playing most matches in ODI history as a captain who has also served as a wicket-keeper (200).
      • Holds the record of most stumpings (120) by any wicket-keeper in ODIs and is currently the only keeper to pass 100 stumpings.
      • First Indian wicket-keeper to take 300 ODI catches and fourth wicket-keeper overall to do so
  2. Hockey
    1. Dhyan Chand
      • Dhyan Chand is known as 'The Wizard', or 'The Magician', for his immaculate ball control.
      • He enrolled himself in the army at the young age of 16, much like his father. Inspiring!
      • Dhyan played various army hockey competitions and regimental games between 1922-1926; making the game his own in a flash.
      • Practicing with the stick during the night after his duty hours, Dhyan earned himself the name of 'Dhyan Chand'. For the unversed, Chand in Hindi means Moon.
      • Dhyan Chand made the Indian Army hockey team for their tour of New Zealand in 1926; impressing one and all with his energy and understanding of the game.
    2. Roop Singh
      • A street in Munich was named after him post his exploits at the Berlin 1936 Summer Games.
      • The Hockey stadium in Gwalior was named after him. Later, this stadium was converted to a cricket stadium.
      • In 2009, the Madhya Pradesh government named the state’s lifetime achievement award for sports after him.
      • In 2012, prior to London Olympics, the Watford High Street was named after Captain Roop Singh.
  3. Football
    1. Lionel Messi
      • He won the 2021 Copa del Rey and scored a brace in a 4-0 final win against Athletic Bilbao. He lifted the 35th and final trophy in his time with the Blaugrana.
      • 50 goals in the league, 14 in the UEFA Champions League, three in the Copa del Rey and six in other competitions. He finished with a ridiculous 73 goals in 60 games in all competitions.
      • He broke the record for the most goals in a single season in European football. Messi overtook Gerd Muller’s tally of 67 in the 1972-73 season.
    2. Cristiano Ronaldo
      • 7 goals were scored in the Club World Cup
      • Four goals have been scored in the Club World Cup Finals (shared with Lionel Messi)
      • 4 (2008, 2014, 2016, 2017) Club World Cup trophies (shared with Toni Kroos)
      • Only player in history to score in three Club World Cups: 2008, 2016, and 2017. (shared with Lionel Messi)
      • Only player to win two Golden Boots at the Club World Cup.
  4. Basketball
    1. Tim Duncan
      • He is a four-time NBA champion, two-time NBA MVP, three-time NBA Finals MVP, and NBA Rookie of the Year.
      • He is a 14-time NBA All-Star and the only player in NBA history to be selected to both All-NBA and All-Defensive Teams during each of his first 13 seasons.
    2. Bill Russell
      • First Black head coach in NBA history
      • 1956 Summer Olympics gold medal
      • 21,620 career rebounds, second-most all time
  5. Badminton
    1. Nandu Natekar
      • Nandu Natekar is considered the “God of Badminton”, Nandu Natekar was one of the most popular and prominent figures in the history of Badminton. In his 15-year career in Badminton, he managed to win over 100 national and international titles.
    2. Parupalli Kashyap
      • Parupalli Kashyap is an Indian badminton player and currently, the top-ranked Indian male badminton player. He is also the number one ranked Indian badminton player in male singles. Having reached the quarterfinal of the London Olympics 2012, he missed winning a medal. But Parupalli is one of the strongest and most famous Indian badminton players.
